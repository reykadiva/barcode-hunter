## 1. Brand Philosophy

### 1.1 Why Scan Chan Exists as a Brand

Scan Chan exists because the world has enough apps that demand attention, create urgency, and make people feel anxious about what they have not done.

Scan Chan exists to be the opposite.

It exists to prove that a digital experience can be meaningful without being addictive. That a game can be rewarding without being stressful. That a product can care about its user without manipulating them.

Scan Chan exists to create a small, warm, quiet place in someone's day — a moment of genuine comfort that does not require anything in return.

### 1.2 Brand Mission

**To create the most emotionally honest companion experience in gaming.**

Not the most addictive. Not the most engaging. Not the most monetizable. The most emotionally honest.

Every decision the brand makes — from visual design to marketing copy to merchandise — must serve this mission. If a decision increases engagement but compromises emotional honesty, it is rejected.

### 1.3 Brand Promise

The Scan Chan brand makes one promise to every person who encounters it:

> **"You will feel a little better after spending time with us."**

This promise applies to:

- Opening the app for thirty seconds
- Browsing the website for the first time
- Seeing a screenshot on social media
- Holding a physical plushie
- Reading a marketing email
- Watching a trailer

Every touchpoint must deliver on this promise. If someone encounters the brand and does not feel slightly warmer, slightly calmer, or slightly more at ease — the brand has failed at that moment.

### 1.4 Brand Personality

If Scan Chan were a person introducing themselves at a gathering, they would not be loud, would not dominate the conversation, would not try to impress anyone. They would be the person sitting comfortably in a corner with a warm drink, who listens more than they speak, who smiles genuinely when someone approaches, and who makes everyone around them feel slightly more relaxed.

The brand personality is:

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Warm** | Radiates quiet comfort without performing it |
| **Genuine** | Never pretends to be something it is not |
| **Patient** | Never rushes, never pressures, never creates urgency |
| **Observant** | Notices details, remembers preferences, seems aware |
| **Quietly funny** | Has a dry, gentle humor — never loud, never sarcastic |
| **Grateful** | Appreciates every interaction without being sycophantic |
| **Independent** | Has its own identity and does not shift to please others |
| **Timeless** | Does not chase trends, does not feel dated |

### 1.5 Emotional Positioning

Scan Chan occupies a specific emotional position in the market that no other product currently holds:

**The intersection of cozy gaming, genuine companionship, and real-world interaction.**

- Unlike Neko Atsume: Scan Chan creates a one-to-one relationship, not a collection
- Unlike Tamagotchi: Scan Chan never punishes absence or creates urgency
- Unlike Animal Crossing: Scan Chan connects to the real world through barcode scanning
- Unlike typical mobile pets: Scan Chan never manipulates through guilt or obligation

The emotional positioning is: **the companion who is genuinely glad you exist, without needing anything from you.**

### 1.6 Long-Term Vision

Scan Chan is being built to last a decade or more.

The brand vision for the next ten years:

| Horizon | Vision |
|---------|--------|
| **Year 1-2** | Establish the core experience. One cat, one room, one warm relationship. Build a community of players who return daily because they want to, not because they have to. |
| **Year 3-5** | Expand the world. Furniture, seasonal content, deeper personality, richer environments. The cat becomes a recognizable character — not just in the game, but in the broader culture of cozy gaming. |
| **Year 5-10** | Become a lasting brand. Merchandise, collaborations, possible new companion species, possible animated content. Scan Chan is recognized alongside LINE Friends, Sumikko Gurashi, and Molang as a character brand with genuine emotional depth. |

### 1.7 What Scan Chan Is Not

The brand is defined as much by what it refuses to be as by what it is.

Scan Chan is NOT:

- A hyper-casual mobile game designed for maximum session time
- A gacha game with pay-to-win mechanics
- A social media platform disguised as a game
- A productivity tool with gamification bolted on
- A children's app (it is for everyone, but designed with adult emotional intelligence)
- A data-harvesting operation disguised as a companion
- A brand that uses fear of missing out (FOMO) as a retention strategy
- A brand that treats its players as metrics rather than people

## 2. Brand Values

### 2.1 Immutable Values

These values cannot change. They cannot be compromised for revenue, for growth, for trends, or for convenience. They are the foundation of every decision the brand makes.

#### Value 1: Emotional Honesty

Scan Chan never pretends to feel something it does not. The brand does not manufacture urgency. It does not create artificial scarcity. It does not simulate anxiety to drive engagement. Every emotion the brand communicates — in the game, in marketing, in merchandise — is genuine and earned.

#### Value 2: Unconditional Warmth

The brand radiates warmth without conditions. It does not matter how long the player has been away. It does not matter how many products they have scanned. It does not matter whether they are a new player or a veteran. The warmth is constant, quiet, and freely given.

#### Value 3: Respect for the Player

The brand respects the player's time, intelligence, emotions, and autonomy. It never condescends. It never manipulates. It never assumes the player owes it anything. Every interaction is voluntary, and every voluntary interaction is rewarded with genuine delight.

#### Value 4: Craftsmanship Over Speed

The brand values quality over quantity, depth over breadth, and patience over urgency. Every pixel, every word, every animation is considered. Nothing ships that does not meet the brand's quality standard. The brand would rather delay a feature than release something that feels rushed or generic.

#### Value 5: Timelessness Over Trendiness

The brand does not chase trends. It does not incorporate memes. It does not redesign itself to match the current aesthetic of social media. It builds things that will feel right in five years, not just today. The brand draws from timeless emotional truths — comfort, companionship, warmth, peace — that do not expire.

#### Value 6: Gentle Independence

The brand has its own identity. It does not shift its personality to match the platform it appears on. A tweet from Scan Chan feels like Scan Chan. A plushie feels like Scan Chan. A Steam page feels like Scan Chan. The brand is consistent because it knows who it is.

### 2.2 What Scan Chan Always Is

- **A safe place** — every interaction is emotionally safe
- **A quiet presence** — never loud, never overwhelming
- **A patient companion** — always willing to wait
- **A small world** — intimate, personal, never sprawling
- **A warm memory** — every encounter should leave the player feeling slightly better
- **A crafted experience** — every detail is intentional
- **An honest relationship** — no manipulation, no deception

### 2.3 What Scan Chan Must Never Become

- **A notification machine** — never nags, never spams, never creates artificial urgency
- **A guilt engine** — never makes the player feel bad about their behavior
- **A generic brand** — never becomes indistinguishable from any other cozy game
- **A corporate voice** — never sounds like a marketing department wrote it
- **A trend-chaser** — never redesigns itself to match what is currently popular
- **A paywall garden** — never locks warmth behind a purchase
- **A data operation** — never treats player information as a product
- **A loud presence** — never becomes noisy, busy, or overwhelming
- **A disposable experience** — never feels like something you play once and forget

## 3. Brand Voice

### 3.1 Voice Philosophy

The Scan Chan brand voice is the written personality of the product. It appears in every piece of text the player encounters — from button labels to error messages to marketing copy.

The voice is: **warm, gentle, quietly observant, slightly poetic, never performative.**

It reads like a friend who writes thoughtful letters — not like a brand trying to seem relatable.

### 3.2 Writing Style

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Simple words** | Use everyday language. Never use jargon, buzzwords, or corporate speak. |
| **Short sentences** | Prefer brevity. One thought per sentence. Let silence do the work. |
| **Warm tone** | Every sentence should feel like it was written by someone who cares. |
| **Quiet humor** | Occasional gentle wit. Never sarcasm. Never memes. Never edgy. |
| **Present tense** | Write about what is happening now. Creates immediacy and presence. |
| **Second person** | Address the player directly ("you"). Creates intimacy. |
| **No exclamation marks** | Or use them extremely sparingly. Excitement is shown through content, not punctuation. |
| **No ALL CAPS** | Never shout. The brand speaks softly. |
| **No emoji overload** | One emoji at most, used rarely and intentionally. Never as decoration. |

### 3.3 Tone by Context

The tone shifts subtly depending on the context, but the underlying warmth never changes.

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| **Greeting** | Warm, welcoming, calm | "Welcome back. Your companion has been waiting." |
| **Instruction** | Gentle, patient, clear | "Point your camera at any barcode to share something new." |
| **Success** | Quietly celebratory, grateful | "A new discovery. Your companion seems pleased." |
| **Error** | Reassuring, helpful, calm | "That did not quite work. Let us try again." |
| **Loading** | Patient, poetic, brief | "Preparing a warm spot..." |
| **Empty state** | Inviting, hopeful, gentle | "Nothing here yet. Your story begins with a single scan." |
| **Farewell** | Warm, peaceful, trusting | "Until next time. Your companion will be here." |
| **Marketing** | Warm, honest, inviting | "A small companion is waiting for you." |
| **Notification** | Gentle, never urgent, never demanding | "Your companion found a sunny spot." |

### 3.4 Vocabulary

The brand uses a specific vocabulary that reinforces its identity.

**Preferred words**: companion, warmth, quiet, gentle, soft, cozy, home, room, story, together, waiting, welcome, calm, peaceful, small, curious, care

**Banned words**: user, engage, retention, grind, addict, addictive, epic, legendary (unless referring to evolution stage), free-to-play, monetize, conversion, funnel, optimize, hack, exploit, urgent, hurry, last chance, limited time, don't miss out, act now

### 3.5 Microcopy Guidelines

Every piece of microcopy must pass the warmth test: "Does this feel like it was written by someone who genuinely cares about the person reading it?"

**Button labels**:

| Instead of | Write |
|------------|-------|
| "Submit" | "Continue" or "Let's go" |
| "Cancel" | "Not now" or "Go back" |
| "Delete" | "Remove" or "Let this go" |
| "Confirm" | "Yes, please" or "That's right" |
| "Sign Up" | "Begin your story" or "Join" |
| "Get Started" | "Meet your companion" |
| "Subscribe" | "Stay connected" |
| "Buy Now" | Never used. The brand does not use pressure language. |

**Form labels**:

| Instead of | Write |
|------------|-------|
| "Username" | "Your name" |
| "Password" | "Your secret" (only if appropriate for context) |
| "Email" | "Your email" |
| "Required field" | "We need this one" |

### 3.6 Notification Philosophy

Notifications are the most sensitive touchpoint. A bad notification breaks the brand promise. A good notification delivers warmth.

**Rules**:

- Never more than 1 notification per day (unless the player explicitly requests more)
- Never use urgency language ("hurry", "last chance", "your pet is starving")
- Never guilt the player ("your companion misses you")
- Always frame notifications as gentle observations, not demands
- Notifications should feel like a friend sending a quiet message, not a product trying to re-engage

**Good notifications**:

- "Your companion found a sunny spot by the window."
- "A new day. Your companion is curious about what you'll discover."
- "The room feels peaceful today."
- "Your companion seems content."

**Banned notification patterns**:

- "Your pet is hungry! Come feed them now!"
- "You haven't visited in 3 days..."
- "Don't miss today's daily missions!"
- "Your streak is about to expire!"
- "URGENT: Your companion needs you!"

### 3.7 Empty States

Empty states are opportunities to set the emotional tone. They should never feel broken, clinical, or lonely.

**Empty state philosophy**: Every empty state is a gentle invitation. It tells a story about what will be here, and makes the player look forward to filling it.

| Context | Empty State Text |
|---------|-----------------|
| No scan history | "No discoveries yet. Every scan is a new story." |
| No achievements | "No milestones yet. Your journey is just beginning." |
| No products | "An empty shelf. Waiting to be filled with discoveries." |
| No missions | "All caught up. Your companion is enjoying the quiet." |
| No collection | "A blank scrapbook. Your collection starts with a single scan." |

### 3.8 Loading Messages

Loading messages should feel like the brand is quietly preparing something, not like a machine processing data.

| Message | Context |
|---------|---------|
| "Warming up a cozy spot..." | General loading |
| "Your companion is stretching..." | App startup |
| "Tidying the room..." | Data refresh |
| "Looking for something interesting..." | Search |
| "Almost ready..." | Final loading stage |

### 3.9 Error Messages

Errors should feel like a gentle apology, not a technical failure.

| Error | Message |
|-------|---------|
| Network error | "The connection dropped. Let us try again when things are calmer." |
| Scan failed | "That barcode was a little shy. Try again or scan something else." |
| Server error | "Something went quiet on our end. We will be back in a moment." |
| Invalid input | "That did not look quite right. Could you check it again?" |
| Timeout | "Things are taking longer than expected. Your companion is patient." |
| 404 page | "This room seems empty. Let us take you back home." |

### 3.10 Success Messages

Success messages should feel like a quiet celebration, not a fireworks display.

| Success | Message |
|---------|---------|
| First scan | "Your first discovery. Your companion is curious." |
| Level up | "A new level. Your companion seems a little more confident." |
| Achievement | "A milestone reached. Well done — both of you." |
| Evolution | "Something has changed. Your companion feels different — in a good way." |
| Mission complete | "That is done. Your companion noticed." |
| Streak milestone | "Day after day. Your companion appreciates the consistency." |

### 3.11 Greeting Messages

The greeting is the first thing the player sees. It must always feel warm, never routine.

| Context | Greeting |
|---------|----------|
| First time | "Hello. Someone is waiting to meet you." |
| Daily return | "Welcome back. The room feels brighter with you here." |
| After 3 days | "It has been a few days. Your companion is glad you are back." |
| After 1 week | "Welcome home. Let us pick up where we left off." |
| After 1 month | "It has been a while. Your companion is still here — and happy to see you." |
| After 6 months | "You came back. That means more than you know." |
| Morning | "Good morning. A new day, a new chapter." |
| Evening | "Good evening. The room is quiet and warm." |
| Night | "Late night. Your companion might be sleepy." |

### 3.12 Farewell Messages

Farewells should feel peaceful, never final.

| Context | Farewell |
|---------|----------|
| Closing app | "Until next time." |
| Long idle | "The room will be here when you return." |
| Completing all missions | "All done for today. Rest well." |
| Before sleep time | "Goodnight. Your companion will keep the room warm." |

### 3.13 Push Notification Philosophy

Push notifications are a privilege, not a right. The brand must earn every notification it sends.

**Core rules**:

- Push notifications are opt-in by default (never opt-out)
- The player can customize frequency (daily, occasional, never)
- Notifications are sent at reasonable hours (never at 3 AM)
- Every notification must deliver genuine warmth — never engagement bait
- The player should feel glad they received the notification, not annoyed

## 3.5 Brand Personality

This section exists to help future artists, designers, and writers deeply understand the emotional identity of Scan Chan by translating it into sensory metaphors.

### 3.5.1 If Scan Chan Were a Person

A quiet librarian in their late twenties who lives in a small apartment with two cats and a collection of houseplants. They write handwritten letters to friends. They prefer tea to coffee. They listen more than they speak. They are not shy — they simply do not need to fill every silence. When you visit them, you always feel slightly more at peace than when you arrived.

They never judge. They never rush. They always have a warm blanket available.

### 3.5.2 If Scan Chan Were a Café

A small, corner café with wooden tables, mismatched ceramic cups, warm pendant lighting, and a bookshelf in the back. The barista knows your name after your second visit. The music is acoustic and quiet — never loud enough to prevent conversation. The windows are large and let in afternoon light. There are no televisions. There is no Wi-Fi password printed on a neon sign. People come here to read, to think, to sit quietly with someone they care about.

The café never plays pop music. It never has a "happy hour" sign. It never rushes you to finish your drink.

### 3.5.3 If Scan Chan Were a Home

A small, one-story cottage with a warm-colored front door, a garden that is tended but not perfect, a living room with a comfortable sofa and too many throw pillows, a kitchen that always smells faintly of something baking, and a window seat where a cat is always sleeping. The floors are wood and slightly creaky. The walls have framed photographs and small watercolors — not expensive art, but meaningful pieces collected over years.

The home never has fluorescent lighting. It never has a smart speaker playing advertisements. It never feels like a showroom.

### 3.5.4 If Scan Chan Were a Season

**Late autumn — the golden hour of the year.**

The leaves have turned amber and gold. The air is cool but not cold. The light is warm and low. There is a gentle breeze. You are wearing a comfortable sweater. There is a warm drink in your hand. The world feels like it is slowing down, preparing for rest, and that slowness feels peaceful rather than sad.

Not spring (too energetic). Not summer (too loud). Not winter (too cold). Late autumn — the season of quiet gratitude.

### 3.5.5 If Scan Chan Were a Smell

Warm vanilla, old paper, cedarwood, and a hint of honey. The smell of a room where someone you trust lives. Not a perfume — a place. The smell that greets you when you walk through the door of a home where you are always welcome.

Not flowers (too decorative). Not citrus (too energetic). Not musk (too heavy). Warm, soft, familiar.

### 3.5.6 If Scan Chan Were a Piece of Music

A solo piano piece — slow tempo, warm chords, gentle dynamics. Something between Erik Satie's *Gymnopédies* and a Studio Ghibli soundtrack. It has moments of quiet melancholy, but the overall feeling is peaceful and slightly nostalgic. It is the kind of music you put on when you want to think, or when you want to stop thinking.

Not jazz (too complex). Not pop (too loud). Not classical orchestral (too grand). Solo piano — intimate, personal, unhurried.

### 3.5.7 If Scan Chan Were Weather

**A warm, overcast afternoon with gentle golden light breaking through the clouds.**

Not sunny (too bright, too energetic). Not rainy (too melancholic). Not stormy (too dramatic). Overcast with golden breaks — the kind of day where the light is soft and even, where everything looks slightly warm, where you feel like the sky itself is being gentle.

## 4. Logo Philosophy

### 4.1 Logo Personality

The logo must communicate the same values as the brand itself: warmth, quiet confidence, gentle personality, and timeless craftsmanship.

It should feel like it was drawn by hand, not generated by a design tool. It should feel like it belongs on a storybook cover, not a tech startup pitch deck.

### 4.2 Shape Language

The logo must use the same shape language as the rest of the brand:

- **Rounded forms** — no sharp corners, no angular elements
- **Organic proportions** — slightly imperfect, human-feeling
- **Soft weight** — medium weight, not too thin (fragile) and not too bold (aggressive)
- **Balanced asymmetry** — centered but not rigidly symmetrical

The logo should incorporate or reference the mascot in some way — either a silhouette, a face, ears, or a subtle cat-like element. The mascot connection makes the logo immediately recognizable and emotionally resonant.

### 4.3 Spacing Philosophy

The logo must breathe. Generous spacing around every element. The logo should never feel cramped or compressed.

**Minimum clear space**: The height of the primary logo element must be maintained as clear space on all sides. Nothing — no text, no images, no borders — should enter this clear space.

### 4.4 Scalability

The logo must work at every size the brand requires:

| Size | Context | Requirements |
|------|---------|--------------|
| 16×16px | Favicon | Silhouette must remain recognizable |
| 32×32px | Tab icon, notification icon | Core shape visible |
| 64×64px | App badge, small avatar | Detail visible |
| 128×128px | Social media avatar | Full logo readable |
| 256×256px | App icon, profile image | All elements clear |
| 512×512px | Store listing, marketing | Full detail, all spacing maintained |
| 1024×1024px | Print, merchandise | Print-ready quality |
| Vector | Large format | Scalable without loss |

### 4.5 Icon Behavior

The icon version of the logo (without wordmark) must:

- Work as a standalone identifier
- Be recognizable at 16×16px
- Maintain the warm color palette
- Never be stretched, rotated, or distorted
- Never be placed on a busy background without a container

### 4.6 Favicon Principles

The favicon is often the first brand touchpoint a player sees (in browser tabs, bookmarks, history). It must:

- Be a simplified version of the logo mark
- Use the signature Honey color
- Remain recognizable at 16×16px
- Never use text (unreadable at this size)
- Feel warm even at this tiny scale

### 4.7 Monochrome Usage

When the logo must appear in monochrome (single-color printing, embossing, engraving):

- The logo must remain recognizable without color
- Use a warm dark tone (dark brown or charcoal) rather than pure black
- Never invert to white on a dark background unless the dark background is warm-toned
- Maintain all spacing and proportions

### 4.8 Dark Mode Usage

In dark mode, the logo adapts:

- Background shifts to Deep Indigo (`#1A1625`) or warm dark surface
- Logo colors shift to lighter, slightly desaturated versions
- The Honey accent becomes Warm Gold (`#E8A84C`)
- The logo must never appear to float on pure black
- A subtle warm glow behind the logo mark is acceptable (suggesting lamp light)

### 4.9 Logo Misuse (Banned)

The following treatments are never permitted:

- Stretching or squashing
- Rotating
- Adding drop shadows not in the original design
- Placing on busy photographic backgrounds without a container
- Recoloring in non-brand colors
- Adding gradients not in the original design
- Placing too close to other elements (violating clear space)
- Using a low-resolution version when high-resolution is available
- Placing the logo on pure black or pure white without warm tinting

## 5. Color Identity

### 5.1 Why These Colors Exist

Every color in the Scan Chan palette was chosen for emotional reasons, not aesthetic ones.

The palette communicates: **warmth, comfort, intimacy, gentleness, and quiet joy.**

The brand deliberately avoids the cool blues, stark whites, and aggressive accent colors that dominate technology products. Scan Chan's colors say "this is a home, not an office."

### 5.2 How Colors Communicate Emotion

| Color | Emotion | Why |
|-------|---------|-----|
| **Honey** (`#F5A623`) | Warmth, care, golden-hour comfort | The signature color. It is the color of sunlight entering a room in late afternoon. It communicates warmth without heat, brightness without harshness. |
| **Cream** (`#FFF8F0`) | Safety, softness, home | The foundation. It replaces pure white, which feels clinical and cold. Cream feels like warm paper, like a page in a well-loved book. |
| **Rose** (`#F472B6`) | Affection, tenderness, gentle love | Not aggressive red. Not childish pink. Rose is the color of a soft blush, of quiet appreciation, of warmth that comes from caring. |
| **Sage** (`#86EFAC`) | Growth, curiosity, natural calm | The color of new leaves. It communicates life and growth without the energy of bright green. It feels organic, natural, unhurried. |
| **Sky** (`#67E8F9`) | Clarity, peace, open space | A gentle blue that feels like a soft sky, not a corporate interface. It provides cool balance against the warm palette. |
| **Lavender** (`#C4B5FD`) | Rest, wisdom, nighttime peace | The color of twilight. It communicates the transition from activity to rest. Used for sleep states and nighttime atmosphere. |

### 5.3 Primary Palette

The primary palette defines the brand's core visual identity. It is used in every touchpoint.

| Name | Hex | Role |
|------|-----|------|
| **Honey** | `#F5A623` | Signature color, primary actions, key highlights |
| **Honey Light** | `#FFD580` | Hover states, soft highlights, warm accents |
| **Honey Dark** | `#C4841D` | Active states, deep warm accents |
| **Cream** | `#FFF8F0` | Page backgrounds, base layer |
| **Warm White** | `#FFFDF9` | Elevated surfaces, card fills |

### 5.4 Secondary Palette

Secondary colors support the primary palette and add emotional variety.

| Name | Hex | Role |
|------|-----|------|
| **Rose** | `#F472B6` | Affection, love-related elements |
| **Rose Light** | `#FBC4DE` | Soft affection backgrounds |
| **Sage** | `#86EFAC` | Growth, positive states, curiosity |
| **Sage Dark** | `#4ADE80` | Active positive states |
| **Sky** | `#67E8F9` | Information, energy, clarity |
| **Sky Dark** | `#22D3EE` | Active information states |

### 5.5 Accent Palette

Accent colors are used sparingly — for special moments, celebrations, and emphasis.

| Name | Hex | Role |
|------|-----|------|
| **Sunbeam** | `#FDE047` | Achievements, celebrations, rare highlights |
| **Lavender** | `#C4B5FD` | Night mode, sleep states, wisdom |
| **Coral** | `#FB923C` | Gentle urgency, attention, low hunger |
| **Petal** | `#FDA4AF` | Gentle warnings, mood indicators |

### 5.6 Seasonal Palettes

The brand palette shifts subtly with the seasons. These shifts are atmospheric, not dramatic.

| Season | Shift | Key Additions |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| **Spring** | Slightly brighter, more green | Soft mint, petal pink, sky blue |
| **Summer** | Warmer, more golden | Deep honey, warm amber, soft peach |
| **Autumn** | Deeper, richer, more amber | Burnt orange, warm brown, deep gold |
| **Winter** | Cooler, softer, more lavender | Deep indigo, soft silver, warm amber (lamp light) |

Seasonal palettes never replace the core palette. They layer on top of it, adding atmospheric variation while the core identity remains recognizable.

### 5.7 Color Hierarchy

Colors are applied in a strict hierarchy to maintain visual consistency:

| Level | Usage | Percentage |
|-------|-------|------------|
| **Foundation** | Cream, Warm White backgrounds | 60-70% of any screen |
| **Primary** | Honey for actions and highlights | 15-20% |
| **Secondary** | Rose, Sage, Sky for meaning | 10-15% |
| **Accent** | Sunbeam, Lavender, Coral, Petal | 3-5% |
| **Text** | Dark brown, warm gray | As needed for readability |

### 5.8 Colors That Must Never Be Used

| Color | Reason |
|-------|--------|
| **Pure black** (`#000000`) | Too harsh, breaks warmth. Use warm dark brown or Deep Indigo. |
| **Pure white** (`#FFFFFF`) as background | Too clinical. Use Cream. |
| **Neon colors** | Too aggressive, breaks the cozy atmosphere. |
| **Corporate blue** (`#0066CC`, `#0052CC`) | Feels like SaaS, enterprise software, banking. |
| **Aggressive red** (`#FF0000`, `#CC0000`) | Too alarming, creates anxiety. Use Coral for gentle urgency. |
| **Pure gray** (`#808080`) | Too cold, too industrial. Use warm grays with brown undertones. |
| **Cyberpunk magenta** (`#FF00FF`) | Completely wrong emotional register. |
| **Tech startup green** (`#00FF00`) | Too digital, too matrix. Use Sage. |

## 5.5 Material Language

### 5.5.1 Surface Materials

Every surface in the Scan Chan world should feel like a material that exists in a warm, lived-in home.

| Material | Where | Why |
|----------|-------|-----|
| **Warm paper** | Backgrounds, cards, panels | Feels like a page in a journal — personal, textured, warm |
| **Soft wood** | Furniture, frames, room elements | Natural, organic, aged with character |
| **Ceramic** | Food bowls, cups, small objects | Handcrafted, slightly imperfect, warm to the touch |
| **Cotton fabric** | Pillows, blankets, curtains | Soft, comfortable, washable, lived-in |
| **Felt** | Small accessories, decorative elements | Soft, matte, warm, slightly fuzzy |

**Materials that are NOT used**:

| Material | Reason |
|----------|--------|
| Glass | Too cold, too reflective, too modern |
| Metal | Too industrial, too hard |
| Glossy plastic | Too cheap, too mass-produced |
| Marble | Too luxurious, too cold |
| Concrete | Too brutal, too urban |
| Carbon fiber | Too tech, too aggressive |

### 5.5.2 Shadow Language

Shadows in Scan Chan are never harsh. They communicate softness, warmth, and comfort.

| Shadow Type | Description | Where |
|-------------|-------------|-------|
| **Soft floating** | Gentle blur, warm brown tint, slight elevation | Cards, panels, modals |
| **Cozy resting** | Very subtle, minimal elevation, warm tint | Buttons resting on surfaces |
| **Ambient warmth** | Large blur, very low opacity, warm color | Overall page depth |
| **Pet shadow** | Small, soft, warm, close to ground | Mascot sprite grounding |

**Shadow rules**:

- Shadows always use warm brown tones, never pure black or pure gray
- Shadow opacity is low (5-15%) — never dramatic
- Shadow blur is generous (12-30px) — always soft
- Shadows suggest gentle elevation, not dramatic floating
- The shadow system must make elements feel like they belong in the same warm room

### 5.5.3 Lighting Language

Lighting sets the emotional atmosphere. It should always feel warm, natural, and comforting.

| Lighting Type | Description | Where |
|---------------|-------------|-------|
| **Warm morning** | Soft golden light from upper-left, long gentle shadows | Default daytime atmosphere |
| **Afternoon amber** | Richer, deeper warmth, slightly shorter shadows | Afternoon/evening transition |
| **Sunset gold** | Deep amber, warm, the "golden hour" feeling | Special moments, celebrations |
| **Rainy day** | Muted, diffused, gray-warm, cozy indoor feeling | Low-energy states, rainy weather |
| **Warm indoor lamp** | Soft amber pool of light against darker surroundings | Dark mode, nighttime |
| **Moonlight** | Cool blue-purple with warm lamp accents | Nighttime atmosphere, sleep states |

**Lighting rules**:

- The primary light source is always warm
- Light comes from the upper-left (natural window light)
- Shadows always fall to the lower-right
- No harsh directional lighting
- No multiple competing light sources
- The lighting should always make the player feel like they are in a warm room

## 6. Typography Identity

### 6.1 Font Philosophy

Typography in Scan Chan communicates warmth through form. The fonts were chosen because they share rounded terminals, warm proportions, and a handcrafted quality that feels personal without being childish.

| Role | Font | Why |
|------|------|-----|
| **Headings** | Fredoka | Rounded, friendly, warm. Feels handcrafted. Has personality without being cartoonish. |
| **Body** | Nunito | Clean, highly readable, warm terminals. Pairs naturally with Fredoka. |
| **Special events** | Pixel font (TBD) | Used only for evolutions, achievements, celebrations. Evokes 8-bit nostalgia. |

### 6.2 Hierarchy

Typography hierarchy communicates importance through weight, size, and color — never through aggressive contrast.

| Level | Font | Weight | Size | Purpose |
|-------|------|--------|------|---------|
| Display | Fredoka | Bold (700) | 40-48px | Logo text, rare celebratory moments |
| H1 | Fredoka | Bold (700) | 32-40px | Page titles |
| H2 | Fredoka | SemiBold (600) | 24-32px | Section headers |
| H3 | Fredoka | Medium (500) | 20-24px | Subsection headers |
| Body | Nunito | Regular (400) | 16px | Primary content |
| Body emphasis | Nunito | SemiBold (600) | 16px | Important inline text |
| Caption | Nunito | Regular (400) | 12-14px | Metadata, timestamps |
| Label | Nunito | SemiBold (600) | 12-14px | Form labels, button text |

### 6.3 Weight Philosophy

Font weights are used meaningfully, not decoratively.

- **Bold (700)**: Only for page titles and celebrations — never for body text
- **SemiBold (600)**: For emphasis and interactive elements
- **Medium (500)**: For subtle emphasis, section headers
- **Regular (400)**: For all body text — comfortable, readable
- **Light (300)**: Rarely used, only for very subtle captions

### 6.4 Spacing

| Element | Spacing Rule |
|---------|-------------|
| Letter spacing | Default or slightly tight for headings (-0.01em) |
| Word spacing | Default — never stretched |
| Line height | 1.5× for body, 1.25× for headings |
| Paragraph spacing | 1.5× line height between paragraphs |

### 6.5 Roundedness

The typography system reinforces the brand's soft, rounded identity:

- All fonts use rounded terminals (Fredoka and Nunito both have this naturally)
- No sharp-edged or angular typefaces
- No serif fonts (too formal, too traditional)
- No condensed fonts (too tight, too corporate)
- No decorative display fonts (too performative)

### 6.6 Number Styling

Numbers carry gameplay significance and require special treatment.

| Context | Treatment |
|---------|-----------|
| XP gains | Fredoka Bold, Honey color — feels celebratory |
| Level display | Fredoka Bold, larger size — feels like a milestone |
| Stat values | Nunito SemiBold — clean, readable, de-emphasized |
| Counts | Fredoka Medium — friendly, not clinical |
| Dates | Nunito Regular, muted — informational, low emphasis |

### 6.7 Button Labels

Button text must be warm and inviting:

- Use Fredoka, Medium to SemiBold
- Short, action-oriented but not aggressive
- Never use imperative commands ("DO THIS")
- Prefer invitations ("Let's go") over commands ("Submit")

### 6.8 Banned Typography

| Banned Element | Reason |
|----------------|--------|
| System fonts (Arial, Helvetica, Times) | Break the crafted feeling |
| Corporate sans-serifs (Inter, Roboto, SF Pro) | Feel like SaaS |
| Comic Sans | Too childish, undermines quality |
| Script/handwriting fonts | Too decorative, too hard to read |
| Display serifs | Too formal |
| ALL CAPS for emphasis | Too loud — use weight and color instead |
| Underlined text (except links) | Too dated |
| Text shadows on body text | Too decorative |

## 7. Illustration Identity

### 7.1 Illustration Philosophy

Every illustration in Scan Chan must feel like it was drawn by someone who loves the world they are depicting. Illustrations are not decorative — they are atmospheric. They create the feeling of looking into a small, warm, lived-in place.

### 7.2 Illustration Style

| Attribute | Rule |
|-----------|------|
| **Medium** | Pixel art for all in-game elements. Warm illustration for marketing and external assets. |
| **Pixel grid** | 48×48 minimum for mascot, 32×32 for objects, consistent across all assets |
| **Outlines** | Warm brown, never pure black (`#2D2620` or similar) |
| **Shading** | 2-3 tonal values per color area. Flat cel-shaded style. No gradients in pixel art. |
| **Proportions** | Slightly stylized — not realistic, not chibi. A warm middle ground. |
| **Detail level** | Clean, readable, not over-detailed. Every pixel must earn its place. |

### 7.3 Backgrounds

Backgrounds should feel like a room, not a void.

| Context | Background Treatment |
|---------|---------------------|
| **In-game room** | Pixel art walls, floor, window, decorations. Warm lighting. Lived-in feeling. |
| **UI panels** | Cream or warm white with subtle texture (paper grain) |
| **Marketing** | Warm gradient or illustrated scene — never plain white, never stock photography |
| **Social media** | Brand-colored backgrounds with illustrated elements |

### 7.4 Decorations

Decorative elements add life without adding information.

**Approved**:

- Pixel-art plants, books, and small objects
- Floating particles (dust motes, hearts, sparkles)
- Gentle gradient washes
- Subtle texture overlays (paper, fabric)
- Hand-drawn dividers (wavy lines, dotted paths)

**Banned**:

- Geometric abstract patterns
- Neon effects
- 3D rendered elements
- Stock illustrations
- Random gradient blobs

### 7.5 Props

Props in the Scan Chan world must feel like objects in a real home:

- **Food items**: Pixel art, warm colors, slightly stylized. Should look appetizing even as pixels.
- **Furniture**: Warm materials (wood, fabric, ceramic). Proportional to the mascot. Interactive.
- **Toys**: Simple, recognizable, playful. Never complex or technological.
- **Books**: Warm spines, visible titles (if legible at scale), slightly worn.
- **Plants**: Green, alive, slightly imperfect (one leaf slightly bent). Communicates life and care.

### 7.6 Food Illustration

Food is central to the game's mechanic (scanning = feeding). Food illustrations must:

- Look appetizing and warm
- Be recognizable at small sizes (32×32 or 48×48)
- Use warm color tones
- Never look processed or artificial
- Have slight imperfections (a bite taken, steam rising, a crumb nearby)
- Match the pixel-art style of the mascot

### 7.7 Furniture Illustration

Furniture defines the room's personality. Each piece must:

- Match the pixel-art style of the mascot exactly
- Use warm materials (wood, fabric, ceramic)
- Cast consistent shadows matching the room's lighting
- Be proportional to the mascot
- Feel like something a real cat would interact with
- Have a clear pixel-art sprite (32×32 or 64×64)

### 7.8 Clouds

Clouds appear in window views and marketing materials.

- Soft, rounded, puffy — never angular or wispy
- Warm white or cream-colored — never pure white, never gray
- Subtle warm tint from the lighting
- Slow-moving if animated
- Simple pixel shapes (3-5 pixel clusters at standard resolution)

### 7.9 Nature

Natural elements connect Scan Chan to the real world.

| Element | Style |
|---------|-------|
| **Trees** | Simple pixel shapes, warm green, visible trunk, rounded canopy |
| **Flowers** | Small, warm-colored, slightly imperfect. Not botanical-accurate. |
| **Grass** | Warm green, soft texture, not uniform — slight variation |
| **Water** | Warm blue, gentle ripples, never cold or dark |
| **Stars** | Warm yellow-white, soft twinkle, never cold blue-white |
| **Moon** | Warm cream, soft glow, never cold silver |

### 7.10 Particles

Particles add life to the world.

| Particle | Context | Style |

### 11.7 Posters

- Full scene illustrations — mascot in room, warm lighting
- Pixel art style consistent with the game
- Brand color palette
- No promotional text (the poster is art, not an ad)
- Print quality: premium paper, warm tones preserved

### 11.8 Packaging

If merchandise is shipped:

- Packaging uses warm materials (kraft paper, tissue paper in brand colors)
- A small card with a warm message from the brand
- No excessive plastic
- The unboxing should feel like receiving a gift from a friend
- A small sticker or extra included as a surprise

### 11.9 Collector Editions

For special milestones or limited releases:

- Premium materials and craftsmanship
- Numbered editions
- Exclusive illustrations or accessories
- Packaging that feels like a treasure box
- The collector edition should feel like owning a piece of Scan Chan history

## 12. Future Expansion

### 12.1 Philosophy

As Scan Chan grows, the brand must remain recognizable. Every expansion must feel like a natural extension of the same warm world — not a departure.

### 12.2 Furniture System

- All furniture matches the pixel-art warm aesthetic
- Furniture defines the room's personality over time
- The mascot interacts with every piece of furniture
- Furniture is earned or crafted, not bought with aggressive monetization

- Events are celebrations, not limited-time obligations
- The mascot participates through accessories and behaviors
- Events never create FOMO — they return annually

### 12.4 Mini-Games

- Mini-games feature the mascot as a companion, not a game piece
- Mini-games are relaxing, not competitive
- Mini-games reinforce the brand's calm, cozy identity
- Rewards from mini-games enhance the room, not create pay-to-win dynamics

### 12.5 Collaborations

- Collaborations must share the brand's values
- The Scan Chan mascot never changes its appearance to match a collaborator
- Collaborations add warmth, not commercial energy
- Every collaboration must pass the brand checklist

### 12.6 Seasonal Updates

- Seasonal updates shift the atmosphere subtly
- The core palette remains — seasonal colors layer on top
- The mascot may wear a subtle seasonal accessory
- The room decorations change with the season
- Seasonal content never replaces the core identity

### 12.7 New Pets (Future)

If additional companion species are introduced:

- Each species must follow the Character Bible's emotional design principles
- The visual style must remain consistent (pixel art, warm palette)
- New species are companions, not collectibles
- Each species has its own character identity document

### 12.8 Cloud Save

- Player data is protected with the same respect the brand shows in all interactions
- The sync experience is seamless and warm — no technical jargon
- Error states during sync use the brand voice (gentle, reassuring)

### 12.9 Mobile Release

If Scan Chan expands to a dedicated mobile app:

- The visual identity remains identical
- The voice remains identical
- The mascot personality remains identical
- The mobile experience is optimized for touch but never compromises warmth

### 12.10 Console Release

If Scan Chan expands to consoles:

- The brand identity must translate to larger screens without losing intimacy
- The room should feel even more atmospheric on a large display
- Controller interactions should feel gentle and warm
- The brand voice in menus and settings remains consistent

## 12.5 Brand Evolution Rules

### 12.5.1 Philosophy

Brands must evolve to remain relevant. But evolution must never compromise identity. Scan Chan will change over the next decade — but it will always feel like Scan Chan.

### 12.5.2 What May Evolve

These elements may change over time as the brand grows:

| Element | How It May Evolve |
|---------|------------------|
| **UI layouts** | Navigation patterns, screen organization, information density may shift with technology |
| **Animations** | New animations added, existing animations refined, smoother transitions |
| **Navigation** | New navigation patterns as features expand |
| **Features** | New systems, mechanics, and content added |
| **Seasonal themes** | New seasonal decorations, events, and accessories |
| **Pixel resolution** | Base resolution may increase (48×48 → 64×64) for more detail |
| **Typography weights** | Additional weights may be introduced for new contexts |
| **Color accents** | New accent colors may be added for new systems |
| **Sound design** | Audio may expand as the experience grows |
| **Marketing channels** | New platforms, new content formats |
| **Merchandise categories** | New product types as the brand matures |

### 12.5.3 What Must NEVER Change

These elements are the brand's DNA. They must remain constant regardless of trends, technology, or business pressure.

| Element | Why It Must Remain |
|---------|-------------------|
| **Emotional warmth** | The core promise. Without warmth, the brand has no identity. |
| **Mascot philosophy** | The companion is a friend, not a product. This never changes. |
| **Companionship as core** | The relationship between player and companion is the entire brand. |
| **Visual softness** | Rounded shapes, warm colors, gentle lighting. Never sharp, never harsh. |
| **Premium craftsmanship** | Every pixel, every word, every interaction must feel intentional and cared for. |
| **Calm atmosphere** | The brand is a quiet place. It never becomes loud, busy, or overwhelming. |
| **No manipulation** | The brand never uses guilt, fear, urgency, or obligation. Ever. |
| **Honey as signature color** | The warm gold that defines the brand's visual identity. |
| **The brand voice** | Warm, gentle, observant, patient. This voice is the brand's personality. |
| **Respect for the player** | The player is a person, not a metric. This is non-negotiable. |

### 12.5.4 The Ten-Year Test

Before making any significant brand decision, ask:

> **"Will this still feel right in ten years?"**

If the answer is no — if the decision relies on a trend, a meme, a temporary technology, or a short-term growth strategy — it should be reconsidered.

Scan Chan is built to last. Every decision should reflect that.

## 13. Brand Checklist

### 13.1 Mandatory Review Checklist

Every new visual asset, marketing material, UI element, illustration, animation, icon, environment, or brand touchpoint must pass this checklist before approval.

| # | Question | Pass Criteria |
|---|----------|---------------|
| 1 | Does this immediately feel like Scan Chan? | A viewer familiar with the brand should recognize it within 2 seconds without seeing a logo. |
| 2 | Would someone recognize this without seeing the logo? | The color palette, shape language, and atmosphere should be identifiable on their own. |
| 3 | Does this reinforce warmth? | The asset uses warm colors, warm lighting, warm materials, and warm language. |
| 4 | Does this reinforce companionship? | The asset supports the relationship between player and companion — directly or atmospherically. |
| 5 | Does this avoid generic mobile-game aesthetics? | The asset does not resemble typical free-to-play visual patterns (neon colors, aggressive layouts, gacha UI). |
| 6 | Does this avoid AI-slop aesthetics? | The asset does not look like it was generated by a default AI tool — it shows intentionality, craft, and specificity. |
| 7 | Is this timeless? | The asset does not rely on current trends, memes, or temporary cultural moments. |
| 8 | Is this premium? | The asset meets the quality standard of a product by Nintendo, Supercell, or Apple. Every detail is intentional. |
| 9 | Is this emotionally memorable? | The asset creates a feeling the viewer will carry with them — even briefly. |
| 10 | Would this still represent Scan Chan in five years? | The asset will not look dated or irrelevant in the near future. |
| 11 | Does this use the correct color palette? | Honey, Cream, Rose, Sage, Sky, and approved accents only. No banned colors. |
| 12 | Does this use the correct typography? | Fredoka for headings, Nunito for body. No system fonts, no corporate fonts. |
| 13 | Does this use rounded shapes? | No sharp corners, no angular elements, no aggressive shapes. |
| 14 | Does this use warm lighting? | Light source from upper-left, warm brown shadows, amber tones. |
| 15 | Is the mascot (if present) consistent with the Character Bible? | Personality, body language, expression, and proportions all match the Character Bible. |
| 16 | Does the voice (if text is present) match the Brand Voice? | Warm, gentle, observant, patient. No corporate speak, no urgency, no manipulation. |
| 17 | Does this respect the player? | The asset treats the viewer as a person, not a metric or a target. |
| 18 | Does this avoid creating urgency? | No "last chance," no countdown timers, no FOMO language, no pressure. |
| 19 | Would this make someone feel slightly better? | The asset delivers on the brand promise: warmth, comfort, quiet joy. |
| 20 | Does this pass the cozy test? | If you showed this to someone and asked "does this feel cozy?", would they say yes? |

### 13.2 How to Use the Checklist

1. Before approving any brand-related asset, review every question above.
2. If any question fails, the asset must be revised before approval.
3. If an asset fails multiple questions, it must be fundamentally reconsidered.
4. The checklist applies to: UI designs, illustrations, marketing materials, social media posts, merchandise designs, packaging, press assets, screenshots, videos, animations, and any other brand touchpoint.

### 13.3 Escalation

If a proposed asset conflicts with this Brand Book but is believed to be necessary for business reasons:

1. Document the conflict clearly
2. Propose an alternative that satisfies both the business need and the brand identity
3. If no alternative exists, the Brand Book takes priority over convenience
4. The conflict and resolution must be logged for future reference
